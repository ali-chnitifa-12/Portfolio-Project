# Upwork Profile Draft

## Best Positioning

Your strongest Upwork angle is not "junior full-stack developer".
It is:

`React / Next.js Front-End Developer`

That matches your portfolio better, sounds more marketable, and is easier to sell on Upwork.

Use full-stack only as a secondary capability.

## Title Options

Pick one of these:

1. `React / Next.js Front-End Developer | TypeScript | Tailwind CSS`
2. `Frontend Developer | React, Next.js, TypeScript, GSAP`
3. `React Developer | Next.js, Tailwind, Responsive UI, API Integration`
4. `Full-Stack Developer | React, Next.js, Node.js, Laravel`

Recommended:

`React / Next.js Front-End Developer | TypeScript | Tailwind CSS`

## What To Remove

Do not use these on your profile:

- `junior developer`
- `I am still learning`
- `I can do anything`
- long lists of every technology you know
- weak openings like `I am passionate about coding`

## Better First Line

Your first 2 lines matter most. Use this style:

```text
I build fast, modern, and responsive web applications with React, Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS.
I help clients turn designs and ideas into clean, professional, and high-performing interfaces.
```

## Final Recommendations

- Keep your title frontend-focused.
- Keep your overview client-focused, not biography-focused.
- Put React, Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind first.
- Show 3 strong projects instead of many weak ones.
- Use portfolio screenshots with clean covers.
- Add your GitHub and live portfolio where Upwork allows it.
- Avoid calling yourself junior on the profile, even if you are early-career.
